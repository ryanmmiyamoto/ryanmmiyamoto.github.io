Overview

My tenure at the UCSD Department of Psychiatry was my first venture into a computer science related job. I started off as an assistant data manager where I was responsible for handling requests made by other faculty members as well as utilizing PHPMyAdmin and Sharepoint to update the websites content. After a year, my immmediate supervisor moved to a different position and I was left to assume their duties. I am currently in charge of migrating the department's sites and their subsites to newer versions of Joomla, but am also responsible for debugging various site errors and routine site maintenance.



What did I learn?


The client is always right. Though you might know how to code an awesome accordion menu or animated scroll feature, it doesn't matter unless that's what the client wants. In my first site redesign, I researched how to create some really cool and modern web modules, but when I checked in my first iteration with the clients, they shot it down as being too ostentatious for the site's purpose. When I was completely finished with the site, it wasn't something I was completely proud of because in my opinion, it was not as aesthetically pleasing as it could be, which is where my next lesson comes in. Good design is subjective. I may not have been completely satisfied, but the clients were happy with the finished product. As long as the client is happy with the final result and I coded it to the best of my ability while following their requirements, there is no reason I shouldn't be proud of my effort.
